About Me

I am a highly skilled computer science graduate from Rutgers University, currently pursuing a master's degree in cybersecurity at Johns Hopkins University. With a solid foundation in computer science and a deep passion for cybersecurity, I have dedicated my career to advancing the field through innovative research and practical applications.

As the founder of Globalization Cybersecurity LLC, I specialize in generating passive income through advanced cybersecurity solutions and strategic investments in emerging technologies. My expertise lies in leveraging machine learning and artificial intelligence to develop cutting-edge strategies that enhance financial security and drive technological advancement.

My research focuses on various aspects of cybersecurity, including intrusion detection systems, blockchain security, cloud computing security, and financial technology (FinTech) security. I am committed to exploring the latest technological advancements to provide robust and effective cybersecurity solutions.

In addition to my professional work, I am actively seeking publication opportunities to share my research and insights with the broader cybersecurity and technology communities. My recent work includes the manuscript Intrusion Detection Systems: Insights and Innovations in Anomaly Detection, which I co-authored with Joel Coffman and am currently pursuing publication at the 17th ACM Workshop on Artificial Intelligence and Security.

Through my efforts, I aim to contribute significantly to the cybersecurity landscape, ensuring the safety and security of digital assets in an increasingly connected world.
