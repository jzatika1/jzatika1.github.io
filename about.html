About Me

I am J. Anthony Zatika, a dedicated researcher and developer at xAI, where I focus on pioneering advancements in artificial intelligence. I hold a computer science degree from Rutgers University and am currently completing my master's degree in cybersecurity at Johns Hopkins University.

My research is at the intersection of AI and cybersecurity, where I strive to create intelligent systems capable of autonomously defending against and adapting to evolving cyber threats.

Previously, I founded Globalization Cybersecurity LLC as a passive income generator, where I specialized in implementing advanced cybersecurity measures for various industries. My expertise includes cloud computing security and leveraging AI-driven technologies to enhance the resilience and security of digital systems.

My research contributions include exploring the security challenges of cloud computing and developing innovative strategies to protect cloud environments from sophisticated attacks. I am passionate about ensuring the security of digital assets in an increasingly interconnected world.

I am actively engaged in the academic community, seeking to publish and share my research findings. My recent work, co-authored with Joel Coffman, titled Innovations in Intrusion Detection Systems, was presented as a poster at the USENIX Security '24 conference in Philadelphia, PA.

Through my ongoing work, I aim to contribute to the future of AI and cybersecurity, ensuring that the technological advancements we pursue are both innovative and secure.
